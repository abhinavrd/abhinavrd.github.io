A chain or flexible string with no bending stiffness takes the shape of a catenary when its ends are held fixed between two supports. If one end of the string is allowed to fall vertically under gravity while holding the other end fixed, it whips or snaps, and the maximum velocity, acceleration and tension rise rapidly. We analyze the scaling of these quantities with time and find the regularizing length scale.
